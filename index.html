Please Don't Cry

Rapsody

Rapsody is very introspective on this album, with some great production as well. Specifically, 'Asteroids' and '3 A.M.' sound great.

Short n' Sweet

Sabrina Carpenter

Sabrina has had a great year this year and I could not get 'Please Please Please' and 'Espresso' out of my head all year.

NO HANDS

Joey Valence & Brae

NO HANDS is very reminicient of the Beastie Boys, in all the best ways. From the funrock/rap production, to the fun bars and lyricism, it is a great listen all the way through.

Alligator Bites Never Heal

Doechii

While I was not initally a fan, Alligator Bites Never Heal has grown on me throughout the year. Doechii has a very ambitious sound, and I am very excited to see what she does next.

COWBOY CARTER

Beyoncé

COWBOY CARTER has some amazing themes and sounds and most of the album is a master class in keeping within the pop genre while paying homage to country influences.

10

Dark Times

Vince Staples

Dark Times is another phenomenal album fro Vince Staples. He has a way of creating the perfect chorus that will always get stuck in your head and keep you coming back. The production is exciting when it needs to be, contrasting Vince's fairly monotone delivery, leading to an amazing final product. Black&Blue is a perfect example of this, with a very busy production, Vince doesn't overdo his delivery, which could have lead to an overly busy song. Additionally, 'Justin' lets Staples show off his story telling skills, if it was only longer. Finally, "Radio"' somehow gives me nostalgia for times that I haven't experienced in full, before streaming services were the main way to listen to music. While the convinience is unmatched, there is something unifying about listening to the radio, calling in a song for a girl, and knowing that she would be listening at the same time as you.
